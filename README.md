### about

I'm a master's student and researcher working at the intersection of **statistical machine learning** and **computational astrophysics**. My work focuses on building uncertainty-aware ML systems that remain reliable when deployed in noisy, real-world physical environments — environments where standard confidence estimates fail.

My current research applies **conformal prediction** to gravitational wave detection pipelines, addressing the distributional gap between synthetic training data and real LIGO detector noise. Prior work identified the *Evaluation Paradox* in physics-constrained time series — demonstrating that standard data splits produce invalid model selection by masking temporal dependencies.

Long-term, I'm building toward **black hole physics**, particle astrophysics, and the information paradox.
